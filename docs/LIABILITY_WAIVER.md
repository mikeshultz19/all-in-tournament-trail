<a id="agreement-to-participate"></a>
## 1. Agreement to Participate

Participation in an All-In Tournament Trail (AITT) tournament, official
practice, registration activity, launch, weigh-in, award presentation, or
closely related event activity is voluntary.

Acceptance of this waiver is a condition of participation. The participant
confirms that the full waiver was available to read before registration and
that the participant had an opportunity to review it and ask questions.

The participant may decline this waiver by choosing not to register or
participate. Visiting the AITT website or opening this page does not constitute
acceptance.

To the extent legally appropriate, this waiver applies to tournament-related
activities occurring before, during, and immediately after an AITT event.

<a id="acknowledgment-of-inherent-risks"></a>
## 2. Acknowledgment of Inherent Risks

Competitive fishing, boating, launching, trailering, and related tournament
activities involve serious risks that cannot be completely eliminated. These
risks include, but are not limited to:

- Boating accidents; collisions with boats, docks, structures, debris, or
  submerged objects; capsizing; falling overboard; and drowning
- Slips, trips, falls, burns, cuts, puncture wounds, and injuries involving
  hooks, lures, rods, knives, or other fishing and boating equipment
- Severe weather, lightning, high winds, waves, rough water, rapidly changing
  conditions, extreme heat, cold exposure, and limited visibility
- Wildlife encounters, underwater hazards, remote locations, and delays in
  emergency response
- Fuel-related hazards, fire, mechanical or electrical failure, engine
  failure, battery failure, steering failure, and communications failure
- Navigation errors, route-selection errors, unsafe speeds, misjudged water
  depth, and incorrect decisions about whether to launch or continue
- Actions, omissions, negligence, or unsafe decisions of other participants,
  recreational boaters, spectators, property owners, or members of the public
- Theft, loss, or damage involving boats, motors, vehicles, trailers,
  electronics, fishing equipment, and personal property
- Serious bodily injury, permanent disability, and death

This list is illustrative, not exhaustive. Known and unknown risks may arise
from the location, weather, equipment, participant conduct, third-party
conduct, or the nature of tournament participation.

<a id="voluntary-assumption-of-risk"></a>
## 3. Voluntary Assumption of Risk

The participant understands these risks, voluntarily chooses to participate,
and knowingly accepts the inherent and reasonably foreseeable risks of
competitive fishing and boating to the fullest extent permitted by applicable
law. Risks may remain even when reasonable safety precautions are followed.

The participant accepts responsibility for risks arising from the
participant's own actions, omissions, judgment, errors, negligence, and unsafe
decisions. The participant also accepts the possibility that another
participant or third party may act negligently or make an unsafe decision.

Tournament Officials cannot observe, supervise, direct, or control every
participant or every situation occurring on the water. Each participant is
responsible for decisions made before, during, and immediately after
competition, including decisions involving:

- Whether to launch, continue fishing, suspend participation, withdraw, or
  seek shelter
- Weather avoidance, water conditions, navigation, route selection, vessel
  speed, and distance from safe harbor
- Boat and equipment condition, fuel planning, communications, emergency
  response, and personal safety

<a id="participant-responsibility-and-independent-judgment"></a>
## 4. Participant Responsibility and Independent Judgment

Each participant is responsible for:

- Personal conduct and on-the-water decisions
- Safe and lawful vessel operation and maintaining control of the vessel
- Determining whether conditions are safe for the participant's boat,
  equipment, experience, health, and abilities
- Selecting safe routes and speeds and avoiding known or reasonably apparent
  hazards
- Complying with federal, state, and local law, boating and fishing
  regulations, and site requirements
- Confirming that required safety equipment is present, accessible, and
  functional
- Deciding whether to launch, continue, suspend, seek shelter, or withdraw
  from competition
- The conduct of a teammate, passenger, or permitted occupant to the extent
  allowed by law

**No tournament schedule, check-in deadline, prize opportunity, rule,
announcement, instruction, or competitive consideration requires a
participant to operate a vessel unsafely or continue participating in unsafe
conditions. Participants must place safety ahead of competition.**

AITT does not make an individualized judgment that a particular participant,
vessel, route, speed, location, or weather condition is safe for that
participant. General tournament information does not replace the
participant's independent judgment.

<a id="boat-operator-and-vessel-responsibility"></a>
## 5. Boat Operator and Vessel Responsibility

The boat operator is responsible for ensuring that the vessel is legally
registered where required, seaworthy, properly maintained, legally operated,
and suitable for reasonably expected conditions.

Before and during participation, the operator is responsible for the
condition and sufficiency of:

- Hull, engine, steering, throttle, electrical systems, and batteries
- Navigation lights, fire extinguishers, throwable flotation devices, and
  personal flotation devices
- Engine emergency-stop kill-switch systems
- Fuel, communications equipment, and other required safety equipment
- Insurance where required by law or applicable agreement

Life jackets and emergency kill switches must be used in accordance with
applicable law and the [Official Tournament Rules](/rules).

Each boat operator remains responsible for safe vessel operation regardless
of tournament standings, weather, time constraints, check-in deadlines, or
fishing conditions. An AITT inspection, boat check, rule-enforcement action,
or failure to identify a defect does not guarantee vessel safety or transfer
responsibility for the vessel to AITT.

<a id="weather-and-water-conditions"></a>
## 6. Weather and Water Conditions

Weather and water conditions can change rapidly. Forecasts, observations, and
provider data may be delayed, incomplete, or inaccurate. Estimated Safe Light
is a planning estimate and does not guarantee safe visibility, navigability,
water, weather, or operating conditions.

AITT may delay, modify, shorten, stop, postpone, cancel, or reschedule an event
for safety reasons. AITT cannot continuously monitor every location,
participant, route, or developing condition. The absence of an official delay,
warning, or cancellation does not mean conditions are safe for every
participant or vessel.

Each participant must independently evaluate current conditions and make
conservative safety decisions based on the participant's vessel, equipment,
experience, abilities, and location. Launch and continuation decisions remain
with each participant.

<a id="medical-fitness-and-personal-condition"></a>
## 7. Medical Fitness and Personal Condition

The participant believes they are physically and mentally capable of
participating. The participant is responsible for considering any medical
condition, medication, impairment, fatigue, dehydration, heat illness, cold
exposure, or other limitation and for obtaining medical advice when
appropriate.

The participant will not participate while impaired by alcohol, illegal
drugs, or any medication or substance that makes participation unsafe. This
waiver does not require disclosure of private medical information not
otherwise required by an approved AITT process.

The participant remains responsible for personal medical expenses arising
from participation, except where responsibility cannot legally be assigned or
waived.

<a id="emergency-medical-treatment"></a>
## 8. Emergency Medical Treatment

If the participant is unable to provide consent during an emergency, the
participant authorizes Tournament Officials or other persons at the scene to
request reasonable emergency assistance, transportation, and medical
treatment.

AITT does not guarantee the availability, timing, adequacy, or outcome of an
emergency response. The participant remains financially responsible for
medical transportation, treatment, hospitalization, and related expenses to
the extent permitted by law.

This authorization does not require any person to provide care beyond their
training and does not create a duty beyond what applicable law requires.

## 9. Release of Liability

owners, officers, directors, Tournament Directors, employees, contractors,
volunteers, scorekeepers, officials, sponsors, partners, host facilities,
property owners, launch facilities, affiliated organizations, agents,
representatives, successors, and assigns.

To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the participant releases
the Released Parties from claims arising from participation in an AITT event
and associated with inherent risks; boating accidents; collisions; falls;
drowning; weather; equipment failure; property damage; emergency response;
the actions of participants or third parties; and the participant's own
decisions, errors, omissions, negligence, unsafe choices, navigation, speed,
route selection, failure to discontinue participation, failure to seek
shelter, or failure to use appropriate safety equipment.

<a id="indemnification-and-responsibility-for-participant-conduct"></a>
## 10. Indemnification and Responsibility for Participant Conduct

To the extent permitted by applicable law, the participant accepts
responsibility for claims, losses, liabilities, or property damage caused by
the participant's unlawful or reckless conduct, negligent vessel operation,
violation of the Official Tournament Rules, damage to another person's
property, or failure to comply with boating or fishing laws.

Any indemnification obligation is intended to be reasonable, legally
permissible, and limited to loss caused by the participant's own conduct. It is
not intended to create an unlimited obligation or cover conduct that cannot
legally be shifted to the participant.

<a id="property-loss-and-damage"></a>
## 11. Property Loss and Damage

To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, AITT is not responsible for
theft, loss, or damage involving boats, motors, electronics, fishing
equipment, vehicles, trailers, personal belongings, phones, cameras, cash, or
other property used, carried, parked, stored, or left at an AITT activity.

Each participant is responsible for securing, maintaining, and insuring their
own property. This provision does not excuse conduct or waive a right that
cannot legally be waived.

<a id="conduct-of-other-participants-and-third-parties"></a>
## 12. Conduct of Other Participants and Third Parties

AITT cannot guarantee the conduct, judgment, skill, equipment, or competence
of another participant. AITT also cannot control recreational boaters,
spectators, property owners, launch users, or other third parties.

To the extent allowed by law, the participant accepts risks created by the
actions, errors, omissions, negligence, or unsafe decisions of others.
Participants must remain alert and take reasonable action to avoid foreseeable
hazards created by other people or vessels.

<a id="compliance-with-laws-and-tournament-rules"></a>
## 13. Compliance with Laws and Tournament Rules

The participant agrees to comply with:

- The [Official Tournament Rules](/rules)
- Federal, state, and local law
- Boating and fishing regulations
- Site, marina, property, and launch-facility rules
- Lawful instructions from public authorities and Tournament Officials

A violation may result in a penalty, loss of weight, disqualification,
suspension, removal from an event, loss of eligibility, or referral to the
appropriate authority, consistent with the Official Tournament Rules and
applicable law. This waiver does not create a specific tournament penalty not
otherwise established by the rules.

<a id="no-guarantee-of-safety"></a>
## 14. No Guarantee of Safety

AITT promotes safety but cannot guarantee participant safety. The presence of
Tournament Officials does not create continuous supervision or control over
participants on the water.

Safety communications, rules, weather notices, forecasts, Estimated Safe
Light, inspections, boat checks, event changes, and emergency assistance do
not eliminate inherent risks or replace independent participant judgment. Each
participant remains responsible for personal safety and conservative
decision-making.

Nothing in this section permits AITT or any Released Party to disregard a
safety obligation imposed by applicable law.

<a id="photography-video-and-media-release"></a>
## 15. Photography, Video, and Media Release

The participant grants AITT reasonable permission to photograph, film,
livestream, record, and identify the participant in connection with AITT
activities. AITT may use the participant's name, image, likeness, voice,
tournament results, catch, and event participation for:

- The AITT website and social media
- Livestreams and event coverage
- Promotional materials and advertising
- Sponsor communications
- Historical archives and future AITT marketing

No additional compensation is required unless AITT and the participant agree
otherwise in writing. These rights are limited to uses reasonably related to
AITT activities and remain subject to applicable law.

If a minor is permitted to participate, any media permission must be reviewed
and accepted by a parent or legal guardian as required by law and approved AITT
policy.

<a id="minors"></a>
## 16. Minors

AITT's current Official Tournament Rules do not finalize minimum-age or
parental-consent requirements. This section does not independently make a
minor eligible to participate.

If AITT approves participation by a minor, a parent or legal guardian must
review and accept the applicable waiver, consent to the minor's participation,
and accept responsibility to the extent permitted by law. Additional
minor-specific documentation may be required. A minor is not assumed capable
of independently waiving all legal claims.

<a id="electronic-acknowledgment-and-signature"></a>
## 17. Electronic Acknowledgment and Signature

Checking the required registration acknowledgment box is intended to
constitute an electronic signature. By affirmatively selecting it, the
participant confirms that they had access to and reviewed:

- The [Official Tournament Rules](/rules)
- This Participant Liability Waiver and Assumption of Risk
- A Privacy Policy, if one is adopted and presented as part of registration

The checkbox is not preselected. Visiting a policy page, browsing the website,
or beginning a form does not constitute agreement. Electronic acceptance is
intended to have the same effect as a handwritten signature where permitted
by applicable law.

AITT may record the date and time, document versions, participant identity,
registration identifier, acknowledgment status, and related acceptance
metadata.

<a id="document-version-and-recordkeeping"></a>
## 18. Document Version and Recordkeeping

The registration system is designed to capture, once backend integration is
complete:

- Waiver version and Official Tournament Rules version
- Date and time of acknowledgment
- Registration identifier and participant name
- Acknowledgment status and other reasonable audit information

The current registration contract carries this information, but AITT does not
yet claim that the acceptance record is permanently stored. Durable
registration persistence and a trusted server acknowledgment time remain a
future production requirement.

Payment-card information is processed by Square. AITT does not store raw card
numbers, CVV values, magnetic-stripe data, or unencrypted payment credentials.

<a id="severability"></a>
## 20. Severability

If a court or other authority determines that a provision is invalid or
unenforceable, the remaining provisions should remain in effect to the fullest
extent permitted by law.

Where legally permitted, an unenforceable provision may be limited or modified
only to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable. Severability does
not expand any provision beyond what applicable law allows.

<a id="entire-agreement-and-no-oral-modification"></a>
## 21. Entire Agreement and No Oral Modification

This waiver, together with the Official Tournament Rules and applicable
written registration terms, represents the participant's agreement concerning
the subjects it covers.

A tournament-day conversation, informal statement, or verbal comment does not
silently replace this written waiver. Any official waiver modification must be
made by AITT through an authorized written update.

This section does not prevent Tournament Officials from issuing lawful
emergency instructions, safety directions, schedule changes, or rules
clarifications within their authority.

<a id="participant-safety-acknowledgment"></a>
## 22. Participant Safety Acknowledgment

I understand that competitive fishing and boating involve risks that cannot
be completely eliminated.

I acknowledge that I am responsible for my own safety, the safe operation of
my vessel, and the decisions I make while participating in an All-In
Tournament Trail event.

I understand that Tournament Officials cannot continuously supervise me or
guarantee my safety while I am on the water.

I voluntarily accept the risks associated with participation, including risks
arising from accidents, weather, equipment failures, the actions of others,
and my own decisions, errors, omissions, or negligence, to the fullest extent
permitted by applicable law.

I understand that I may withdraw from participation whenever I believe
conditions are unsafe.

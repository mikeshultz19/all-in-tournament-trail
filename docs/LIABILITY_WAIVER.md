# All-In Tournament Trail

## Participant Liability Waiver and Assumption of Risk

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Draft – Pending Legal Review
**Effective Date:** (TBD)

> **Legal review notice:** This document is intended as a draft for review by
> qualified legal counsel before official adoption. It is not legal advice and
> must not be represented as an adopted waiver until that review is complete.

## 1. Agreement

Participation in an All-In Tournament Trail (AITT) tournament, official
practice, weigh-in, or related activity is voluntary.

Participation requires acceptance of this Participant Liability Waiver and
Assumption of Risk. By accepting it, the participant confirms that they have
read and understood its terms and have had the opportunity to ask questions
before participating.

## 2. Assumption of Risk

Bass fishing and tournament activities involve risks that cannot be completely
eliminated. These risks include, but are not limited to:

- Boating accidents
- Slips and falls
- Drowning
- Severe weather and lightning
- Wildlife
- Submerged or unseen objects
- Boat, vehicle, trailer, or equipment failure
- Collisions with boats, docks, land, or other objects
- Cuts, punctures, hooks, and other injuries caused by fishing equipment
- Exposure to extreme heat or cold

Other known or unexpected risks may also arise from travel, launching,
boating, fishing, handling fish, weighing fish, or attending an AITT activity.
The participant voluntarily assumes the risks of participation to the extent
permitted by law.

## 3. Medical Responsibility

The participant certifies that they are physically capable of participating
or have consulted an appropriate medical professional before participating.
The participant is responsible for deciding whether any health condition,
medication, disability, or physical limitation makes participation unsafe.

The participant is responsible for personal medical expenses arising from
participation. AITT, Tournament Directors, and Tournament Officials are not
responsible for those expenses except where responsibility cannot legally be
waived.

## 4. Boat Responsibility

The participant is responsible for ensuring that any boat they own or operate
is safe, properly maintained, legally equipped, and legally operated.

The participant agrees to:

- Follow all applicable federal, state, and local boating laws;
- Follow lake, marina, launch-facility, and Tournament Official instructions;
- Carry required United States Coast Guard safety equipment; and
- Use life jackets and engine emergency-stop kill switches as required by the
  [Official Tournament Rules](/rules) and applicable law.

Nothing in this waiver reduces the boat operator's responsibility for safe
operation or the safety responsibilities of each participant.

## 5. Release of Liability

To the extent permitted by law, the participant releases AITT, its Tournament
Directors, Tournament Officials, volunteers, sponsors, host facilities, and
affiliated organizations from claims for personal injury, death, property
loss, or other damage arising from the ordinary risks of voluntary
participation in an AITT activity.

This release does not apply where a release is prohibited by law. It is not
intended to waive claims that cannot legally be waived or to excuse conduct
for which liability cannot legally be limited.

## 6. Property Damage

The participant accepts responsibility for loss of or damage to their own
property. This includes boats, motors, vehicles, trailers, fishing equipment,
electronics, safety equipment, and personal belongings.

AITT is not responsible for theft, loss, or damage to participant property
except where responsibility cannot legally be excluded.

## 7. Emergency Medical Treatment

If the participant is unable to provide consent during an emergency, the
participant authorizes Tournament Officials or other persons at the scene to
request reasonably necessary emergency medical assistance or transportation.

This authorization does not require any person to provide medical care beyond
their training. The participant remains financially responsible for emergency
services, transportation, treatment, and related medical expenses.

## 8. Photography & Media Release

The participant grants AITT permission to photograph, film, livestream,
record, and identify the participant during registration, official practice,
launch, competition, weigh-in, awards, and related activities.

AITT may use the participant's name, image, likeness, voice, boat, catch, and
tournament results without compensation in:

- The AITT website
- Social media
- Promotional materials
- Advertising
- Future marketing
- Tournament news, results, broadcasts, and archives

AITT will use participant information subject to applicable law and any
adopted privacy policy.

## 9. Rules Acknowledgment

The participant confirms that they have read and agree to comply with the
[Official Tournament Rules](/rules), applicable law, facility requirements,
and instructions from Tournament Officials.

A rules violation may result in a penalty, disqualification, loss of points or
winnings, removal from an event, or loss of future eligibility as permitted by
the Official Tournament Rules.

## 10. Electronic Signature

By checking the required registration acknowledgment box and submitting the
registration, the participant intends to provide an electronic signature and
confirms that they have read and agree to the applicable:

- [Official Tournament Rules](/rules)
- Participant Liability Waiver and Assumption of Risk
- Privacy Policy, when applicable

The participant agrees that this electronic acknowledgment has the same force
and effect as a handwritten signature where permitted by law.

AITT should retain the applicable document versions, registration identifier,
and trusted acknowledgment timestamp as part of the registration record.

## 11. Governing Law

This waiver and disputes arising from it are governed by the laws of the State
of Texas, without overriding any law that must apply regardless of this
provision.

## 12. Severability

If a court or other authority determines that any provision of this waiver is
invalid or unenforceable, that provision will be limited or removed only to
the extent necessary. The remaining provisions will remain in effect to the
extent permitted by law.
